Kuala Terengganu

Kuala Terengganu is the capital of the great state of Terengganu in Malaysia. As part of a trip here you can walk around the town and visit pretty heritage buildings and museums, or you spend time mussel picking and squid fishing.

Pantai Batu Buruk

If you want to find some sand in Kuala Terengganu then head for Pantai Batu Buruk which is one of the most popular beaches in the city.


Chinatown

Kuala Terengganu is blessed with a large Chinese population and with that in mind you can visit the pretty area of Chinatown when you are in the city.

Masjid Terapung

You can sits around 10 kilometers away from the center of Kuala Terengganu.The mosque is an ode to classic Islamic architecture and is painted a brilliant white.

Kelantan

Kelantan in Malaysia will always be any traveller’s dream destination, thanks to its architecture, natural haven, historical places, temples, jungles, paddy fields, rustic fishing villages, and beaches. The state is located in the north-eastern corner of the peninsula, which translates as the “Land of Lightning”.

Min House Camp

Spending the day at Min House Camp. You can do all things outdoors from water sport activities such as kayaking, river bathing, fishing, clam digging, and even firefly watching at night.


Restoran Capital

Breakfast at Restoran Capital in Kota Bharu and try their famous nasi kerabu. This dish is a combination of flavored rice with various Kelantanese curries, and extras including eggs and crunchy crackers for that unique texture.

Kampung Kraftangan

Drop by Kampung Kraftangan “Balai Getam Guri” and witness some local activities such as batik-making or woodcarving. It’s considered as one of the must-sees in Malaysia as one gets to have a deeper knowledge of the locals’ fine handwork.

Penang

Ah, Penang, the Pearl of the Orient. Unless you’ve been living (or travelling!) under a rock for the past few years, chances are you’ve heard of this magnificent city in Malaysia. In fact, in recent times, it’s been ranked by many travel reviews as one of the very best travel destinations not just in the country or the region, but in the whole world!

Street of Harmony

It's one of the locations that better represents Malaysia's multi-cultural unique country. The street is packed with numerous places to serve various religions. It's not unusual to see devotees on and off a weekend or religious holiday.



Cheong Fatt Tze Mansion

If you've caught the movie, you'll recall Michelle Yeoh and Constance Wu's epic mahjong showdown—yes, the scene was filmed at Cheong Fatt Tze Mansion aka The Blue Mansion! Founded in the 1880s by a wealthy Chinese businessman, the building features a mix of lavish West architecture.

Street Mural

Play hide & seek Penang's street murals. I guess it was about late 2009 or early 2010, when my friends began sharing pictures of themselves on Penang streets next to artistic murals. It wasn't long until monitoring these artworks became the next trend in Penang tourism, including for locals!
