Kuala Terengganu

Terengganu is a state that’s not only blessed with beautiful islands, it also has a wide variety of traditional food that is loved throughout the country.

Nasi Dagang

A state representative food, atas tol is tasty and wholesome. The rice bowl, eaten with gulai ikan tongkol, and finely pickled vegetables. What makes rice special is a blend of normal rice and glutinous rice.


Keropok

A popular snack across the country, keropok is made from grounded fish meat and sago. The keropok is available in two different forms; long chewy ones are called lekor while the crispy thinner ones are called keping.


Sata

Sata is a mix of succulent fishmeat marinated with different spices. When the paste is well mixed, it is wrapped in triangular-shaped banana leaves, and grilled over a charcoal fire. It has a nice flavour, perfected with grill smokiness tinge.

Kelantan

Due to Kelantan's relative isolation and largely rural lifestyle, the culture of Kelantanese differs somewhat from Malay culture in the rest of the peninsula. This is reflected in the arts, customs, dialects and cuisine. We have listed out Kelantanese one-of-a-kind, yet mouth-watering foods below.

Nasi Tumpang

Rice with different layers of banana leaf cone covered. Kelantanese titles Nasi Tumpang. Tightly packed with omelette, beef or fish floss, local curry shrimp or fish, sweet sambal gravy and cucumbers. There are 2 ways of eating Nasi Tumpang, using a spoon, chewing it layer by layer or collapse and mixing it all in a mess.

Roti Titab

It looks simple with bread, butter, kaya, egg ingredients. Yet visitors, and even locals, enjoy it, and would return for more. Kopitiam Kita's owner patented Roti Titab to discourage any other stalls trying to sell the same dish and ensure that travellers enjoy the initial flavour of Roti Titab.

Nasi Kak Wok

Nasi Kak Wok is the crispy, tender and served spicy fried chicken split into tiny pieces of gravy curry sauce. The late Kak Wok, real name Mek Wok Bt Hassan was the individual who produced this meal, and until now she transferred the recipe from generation to generation.


Penang

In Penang, everyone is Penang Foodie. We all know where to locate the best food in Penang. Just to let you guys know, we researched on some Penang food blog when writing this guide. We hope this blog will give you a good peek on what to eat in Penang. If you’re travelling to Penang, this guide surely will help you, trust me!

Transfer Road Roti Canai

Transfer road roti canai serves one of the legendary roti canai in town. We’ve featured it in our best breakfast in Penang list. Grab a chair and taste the best roti canai in town with the locals. What’s the must try here? We recommend beef gravy and chicken curry.

Bangkok Lane

Penang style mee goreng is must to eat in the Pearl of the Orient. The uncle is local Indian but speaks fluent hokkien, when he’s in good mood you can spot him showing off his hokkien while cooking. Such a beauty of mixture culture in Penang.


Air Itam Laksa

So here's two lovely laksa stall for you. Air Itam's two deserving laksa try. If visiting Kek Lok Si or Penang Hill, note to visit these two stalls. First, Air Itam Laksa's super-famous. It's still busy as local and visitors enjoy it. Personally, I like it because the broth is rich and the fresh fish.
